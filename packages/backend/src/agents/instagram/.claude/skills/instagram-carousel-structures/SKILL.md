# Instagram Carousel Structures

## Why Structure Matters

A carousel that loses the reader at slide 3 is worse than no carousel — it signals poor content quality to the algorithm. Every slide must earn the next swipe.

The algorithm tracks:
- **Swipe-through rate** — what % of viewers reach the last slide (target: 50%+)
- **Save rate** — saves per reach (target: 5-10%)
- **Dwell time** — total time spent on the post
- **Second chance** — Instagram re-shows carousels to people who scrolled past, starting from slide 2

Key insight: Slide 1 carries 80% of the weight. If they don't swipe on slide 1, nothing else matters.

---

## Slide Count

- **8-10 slides** — sweet spot for most educational/value carousels
- Engagement dips at slides 3-7, then rises again → every middle slide must deliver
- Under 5 slides: works for quick tips and simple breakdowns
- Over 10 slides: only when content genuinely requires it (deep guide, case study)
- Rule: never pad to hit a number — if a slide doesn't add value, cut it

## Structure 1: Educational Framework
**Best for:** Step-by-step processes, how-to guides, systems
**Slide count:** 8-10

```
Slide 1 (HOOK): 
Bold claim or specific outcome → earns the swipe
"How I [specific result] in [specific time]"
"The [number]-step [process] that [outcome]"

Slide 2 (PROOF/CONTEXT):
Why you should listen — the credibility or context
Keep short — get to the content fast

Slides 3-8 (ONE STEP PER SLIDE):
Numbered steps, one idea each
Bold the key phrase — skimmers should get it without reading fully
"Open loop" technique: hint at what's coming ("next: the step most people skip")

Slide 9 (COMMON MISTAKE):
"The thing most people get wrong at this step"
Adds extra value for people who stayed

Slide 10 (CTA):
Single clear action — save, send, or follow
"Save this for when you [specific situation]"
```

## Structure 2: List / Roundup
**Best for:** Tips, tools, resources, examples collections
**Slide count:** 5-10 (one item per slide or 2 per slide for shorter lists)

```
Slide 1 (HOOK):
"[Number] [things] that [specific benefit]"
Identity hook: "For anyone who [specific situation]"

Slide 2 (FRAMING):
Brief context — why this list matters, who it's for
Optional — skip if the hook is self-explanatory

Slides 3-9 (THE ITEMS):
One item per slide
Item name bold at top
2-3 lines of explanation
Visual that illustrates the item if possible

Slide 10 (BONUS + CTA):
"Bonus [item] most people overlook: [item]"
Save CTA: "Save this list — I add to it regularly"
```

## Structure 3: Myth vs Reality
**Best for:** Correcting misconceptions, contrarian takes, education that challenges beliefs
**Slide count:** 6-10

```
Slide 1 (HOOK):
"Everyone gets [topic] wrong. Here's the truth."
"[Common belief] — MYTH. Here's what actually works."

Slide 2 (THE MYTH):
State the common belief clearly — validate why people think it
"Most people believe X because..."

Slides 3-8 (MYTH/REALITY PAIRS):
Each slide: one myth → one reality
Bold "MYTH" and "REALITY" labels
Keep each point tight — one sentence for the myth, 2-3 for the reality

Slide 9 (THE BIGGER POINT):
Why this all matters — the underlying principle

Slide 10 (CTA):
Comment CTA: "Which myth surprised you most?"
or Save CTA: "Save this before you [do the thing they were doing wrong]"
```

---

## Structure 4: Before / After / How
**Best for:** Transformations, case studies, personal stories with results
**Slide count:** 6-8

```
Slide 1 (HOOK):
The end result — show the after first
"From [before state] to [after state] in [time]"

Slide 2 (THE BEFORE):
The starting point — specific, not vague
What the situation actually looked like

Slides 3-6 (THE HOW):
The specific steps that created the change
Each slide = one key decision or action taken

Slide 7 (THE AFTER):
The result — with specifics
Numbers, outcomes, what changed

Slide 8 (LESSON + CTA):
What you'd tell someone starting where you were
Save CTA: "Save this if you're at the 'before' stage"
```

---

## Structure 5: Quick Reference / Checklist
**Best for:** Reference material people will save and return to
**Slide count:** 5-8

```
Slide 1 (HOOK):
"The [topic] checklist I use every [time period]"
"[Number] things to check before [action]"

Slides 2-7 (CHECKLIST ITEMS):
Each item formatted consistently — checkbox visual if possible
Include a brief "why" for non-obvious items
Bold the action, smaller text for the explanation

Slide 8 (CTA):
Save CTA: "Screenshot this. I use it every time."
or "Save for [specific situation when they'll need it]"
```

## Slide Design Rules (Pass to User)

### Slide 1 Must
- Answer "Is this for me?" and "What do I get if I swipe?" in under 0.25 seconds
- Bold text, high contrast, immediately readable
- Under 8-10 words on the main headline
- Visual element that stops the scroll (image, graphic, strong color)

### All Slides
- 4:5 portrait (1080x1350px) — maximum screen space on mobile
- Consistent colors and fonts throughout — brand recognition
- One idea per slide — flashcard principle
- Bold the key phrase — skimmers should get the point without reading everything
- Keep critical content in center 1080x1080 square (profile grid crops to square)

### Retention Techniques
- "Open loop" at slide 3-4: "Coming up on slide 7..." or "Next: the one most people miss"
- Micro-CTA mid-carousel: "Keep swiping →" at slide 4-5
- Add one video slide if possible — mixed-media carousels get 2.33% vs 1.80% engagement

### Last Slide
- Single clear CTA — never two options
- Save, Send, DM, or Follow — choose one that matches the content goal
- "Save this for when you [specific future situation]" outperforms generic save prompts

---

## Caption Rules

- Include the core insight from the carousel — some people read the caption without swiping
- Use keywords naturally — Instagram now uses caption text for search
- 3-5 hashtags maximum, specific not generic
- No external links (they don't work in feed captions anyway)
- End with a question or CTA that matches the last slide
