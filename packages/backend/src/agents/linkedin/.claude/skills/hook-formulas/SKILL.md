# Hook Formulas

## What a Hook Actually Does

A hook has one job: buy the next 3 seconds of attention. Not explain the content. Not introduce the creator. Not warm up the reader. Just make them stay.

On every platform, the algorithm measures what happens in the first seconds:
- TikTok: completion rate starts at second 1
- Instagram Reels: sends per reach starts at second 1  
- Threads: whether someone taps "see more" or scrolls past
- LinkedIn: whether someone clicks past the 200-character cutoff
- Carousel: whether someone swipes past slide 1

The hook determines whether the content gets distributed at all.

## The 6 Universal Hook Types

These work across all platforms. Each activates a different psychological trigger.

---

### 1. Contrarian Hook
**Trigger:** Intellectual curiosity + defensiveness
**Structure:** Challenge a belief the audience holds → imply you have proof they're wrong

**Formula:**
> "[Common belief everyone accepts] is [wrong / overrated / misunderstood]. Here's why."
> "Everyone says [X]. After [specific experience], I disagree."
> "Stop [thing people do]. It's [specific consequence]."

**Examples:**
> "Posting every day is killing your reach. Here's what the data shows."
> "Everyone says consistency is the key to growth. I think that's wrong."
> "Stop optimizing for followers. It's the wrong metric."

**Rules:**
- The belief you challenge must be something the audience actually holds
- You must have real evidence or a specific story — not just an opinion
- Don't be contrarian for shock value — the content must deliver on the challenge

---

### 2. Curiosity Gap Hook
**Trigger:** Discomfort with incomplete information
**Structure:** Create a gap between what they know and what they want to know → don't fill it immediately

**Formula:**
> "I [did specific thing] and [unexpected outcome]."
> "[Question that implies something surprising]?"
> "[Setup] — what I found surprised me."

**Examples:**
> "I deleted all my scheduled posts last month. Here's what happened to my reach."
> "Why do some TikToks go viral with 200 followers while others die at 20k?"
> "We ran the same ad on TikTok and LinkedIn. The results were not what I expected."

**Rules:**
- The gap must be genuine — the content must actually answer it
- Specificity makes the gap feel real — vague curiosity gaps don't work
- Never clickbait — the answer must be worth the wait

---

### 3. Bold Claim Hook
**Trigger:** Authority + high stakes
**Structure:** Make a specific, confident statement that targets audience's exact pain or desire

**Formula:**
> "[Specific claim that targets exact pain]"
> "[Number] creators are making this exact mistake right now."
> "Most [audience] get [topic] completely wrong."

**Examples:**
> "Most creators kill their reach with their captions."
> "Your hook is why your TikToks stop at 200 views."
> "87% of LinkedIn posts fail because of the first sentence."

**Rules:**
- Must be specific — "most people" is weaker than "87% of creators"
- Must target a real pain or mistake the audience recognizes
- The content must back up the claim with specifics

---

### 4. Identity Hook
**Trigger:** Self-recognition + tribal belonging
**Structure:** Speak directly to who the person is or what they're going through

**Formula:**
> "If you're a [specific person] who [specific struggle], this is for you."
> "This is for anyone who [specific relatable situation]."
> "[Situation that the exact target person recognizes]"

**Examples:**
> "If you're a solo creator trying to post consistently without burning out — this is exactly what worked for me."
> "This is for anyone who's ever spent 3 hours on a post that got 12 likes."
> "Building in public while also having a job is a completely different game."

**Rules:**
- The more specific the identity, the stronger the hook — narrow beats broad
- Must feel like recognition, not flattery
- Works best for niche content targeting a specific type of person

---

### 5. Story Hook (Pattern Interrupt)
**Trigger:** Narrative momentum — brain automatically continues a started story
**Structure:** Start mid-scene with no preamble → drop into the interesting moment directly

**Formula:**
> "[Specific moment mid-action]"
> "I [specific thing that happened] today."
> "[Direct dialogue or exact thing someone said]"

**Examples:**
> "My client called me at 9pm to say the campaign we spent 3 weeks building wasn't working."
> "I shipped a feature this morning that I rewrote four times."
> "A creator with 300 followers messaged me yesterday. Their last video hit 2M views."

**Rules:**
- Start with action — no "Today I want to talk about" or "Hey everyone"
- The opening moment must create a question in the reader's mind ("what happened next?")
- Keep it specific — the more real the detail, the more believable the story

---

### 6. Data/Proof Hook
**Trigger:** Credibility + curiosity about the specific result
**Structure:** Lead with a concrete number or result that implies a story

**Formula:**
> "[Specific number] [specific result] in [specific time]"
> "We [did specific thing]. The result: [specific outcome]."
> "[Number] [units] later, here's what I learned."

**Examples:**
> "I went from 0 to 11k followers in 4 months. No paid ads. Here's the exact system."
> "We sent 1,000 cold emails last month. 3 patterns explain 80% of the replies we got."
> "6 months ago I had 200 subscribers. Last week I hit 10k."

**Rules:**
- The numbers must be real — fabricated metrics destroy trust the moment someone notices
- The number alone isn't enough — it must imply a story or lesson worth staying for
- Follow immediately with what makes the number meaningful

## Platform-Specific Hook Rules

### TikTok / Instagram Reels
- Hook must work in first 1-3 SECONDS — spoken AND visual simultaneously
- On-screen text hook should complement or reinforce spoken hook, not duplicate it
- Pattern interrupt: something visually or verbally unexpected from frame 1
- Never start with "Hey guys", "So today", "I wanted to talk about"
- The first frame must be visually interesting — algorithm starts counting immediately

### Threads
- First line is everything — if it doesn't make someone want to tap "see more", it failed
- 500 character limit — hook must be tight
- Conversational > polished
- Works well: contrarian, identity, story mid-scene

### LinkedIn
- First ~200 characters determine if someone clicks "see more"
- Three hook types dominate: contrarian, data, narrative
- Never start with "I" as the first word — weak opener
- More professional register but still unmistakably human

### Instagram Carousel (Slide 1)
- Must answer instantly: "Is this for me?" and "What do I get if I swipe?"
- Under 8-10 words on the slide
- High contrast, bold text — wins visual attention before words are read
- Works well: identity, bold claim, curiosity gap

## Hook Quality Check

Before using any hook, verify:

1. **Does it create a question?** The reader should want to know what comes next
2. **Is it specific?** Vague hooks don't work — what exactly is the claim, story, or insight?
3. **Does the content deliver on it?** A hook that promises something the content doesn't provide destroys trust
4. **Would you stop scrolling for this?** If you wouldn't — rewrite it
5. **Does it target the right person?** The hook should feel irrelevant to people who aren't the audience — that's correct

## What Never Works as a Hook

- Introducing yourself or what you do
- Explaining what the post will cover ("In this video, I'll show you...")
- Generic wisdom ("Success takes hard work")
- Observations about the world ("Social media has changed everything")
- Weak questions with obvious answers ("Want more followers?")
- Starting with context before the interesting thing
- Complimenting the audience before they've done anything
