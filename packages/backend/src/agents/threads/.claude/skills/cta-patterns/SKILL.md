# CTA Patterns for Threads (2026)

## How Threads CTAs Work Differently

On Threads, the algorithm ranks by **reply depth** — the number and quality of replies a post generates. This changes everything about what a CTA should do.

- **Replies > likes** — a CTA that generates a reply is worth 5x a CTA that generates a like
- **External links in post body kill reach** — never include a URL in the post itself (put in first reply if needed)
- **Engagement bait is penalized** — "like if you agree", "drop a ❤️" actively suppress reach
- **Follow CTAs backfire** — they signal desperation and low-quality content; Threads followers come from being interesting, not from asking

One CTA per post. No exceptions. More than one dilutes both and reads as AI.

---

## The 3 CTAs That Actually Work on Threads

---

### 1. Reply Invitation (Highest Priority)
**Why it works:** Replies are the #1 algorithm signal on Threads. A post that generates 10 replies outperforms a post with 200 likes.

**Formula:**
> "[Question that has a real range of valid answers]?"
> "Disagree? Tell me why."
> "What's your experience been with [specific thing]?"
> "Reply with [specific thing] — genuinely curious."

**Examples:**
> "At what point did you stop explaining your prices?"
> "Disagree? I've heard the counter-argument. Make your case."
> "What's the thing you wish someone had told you in month 1?"
> "Reply with your niche — I want to see if this pattern holds."

**Rules:**
- The question must have a real range of valid answers. "Agree?" is not a question — it's bait.
- "What do you think?" is too vague — always ask something specific
- The question should be something the creator genuinely wants to hear answered
- Questions that invite disagreement generate more replies than questions that invite agreement

---

### 2. Save CTA
**When to use:** Content is educational, a framework, a checklist, or a reference the person will want to return to.
**Best for:** Carousels, LinkedIn posts with frameworks, Threads posts with step-by-step processes

**Formula:**
> "Save this for when you [specific situation where they'll need it]."
> "Screenshot this. You'll want it for [specific moment]."
> "Bookmark this one — it took me [time] to figure out."

**Examples:**
> "Save this for the next time your post isn't getting traction."
> "Screenshot the checklist on slide 8. I use it before every video."

**Why it works:** Saves signal high-value content to the algorithm. Framing it around a specific future moment makes the save feel useful, not transactional.

---

### 2. Send / Share CTA
**When to use:** Content is highly relatable, solves a common problem, or would help someone the reader knows.
**Best for:** Carousels, Reels, TikTok, Threads posts about universal experiences

**Formula:**
> "Send this to [specific person who would benefit]."
> "Tag someone who needs to hear this."
> "If you know a [specific person] dealing with [specific problem] — send this."

**Examples:**
> "Send this to the creator who keeps asking why their posts aren't growing."
> "Tag someone who's been putting off starting their channel."
> "If you know anyone building a solo project, send this."

**Why it works:** Shares and sends are the highest-value algorithm signal on Instagram Reels and Threads. Being specific about who to send it to makes the action feel natural.

---

### 3. Comment CTA
**When to use:** The content ends with a genuine question the creator wants an answer to, or there's a debate worth having.
**Best for:** Threads, LinkedIn, TikTok

**Formula:**
> "What's your take? [Specific question]"
> "[Specific question that has real range of answers]?"
> "Reply with [specific thing] — I'm curious how common this is."

**Examples:**
> "What would you have done? I'm still not sure I made the right call."
> "How are you handling this? Genuinely curious what's working."
> "Reply with your niche — I want to see if this pattern holds across industries."

**Why it works:** Comments signal active engagement. Questions that have a real range of possible answers generate more comments than yes/no questions. Never ask "What do you think?" — too generic. Always ask something specific.

**What to avoid:** "Drop a comment if you found this useful" / "Let me know your thoughts below" — these are lazy and produce low-quality engagement.

---

### 4. Follow CTA
**When to use:** Only when the content is a series, there's more coming, or the creator posts this type of content regularly.
**Best for:** TikTok, Instagram Reels (for growth), LinkedIn

**Formula:**
> "Follow for more [specific type of content] — I post [frequency]."
> "I share [specific thing] every [day/week] — follow so you don't miss it."
> "This is part of a series on [topic] — follow to get the next one."

**Examples:**
> "I document my build in public every week — follow if you want to see how this ends."
> "I post one AI workflow every Tuesday. Follow if you're building with AI."

**Why it works:** A follow CTA is only effective when the audience has a clear reason to follow. "Follow for more content" is too vague. "Follow to see how this project ends" gives a specific reason.

**When NOT to use:** After content that was a one-off insight with no series behind it. An unjustified follow CTA feels desperate.

---

### 5. DM CTA
**When to use:** Creator has something specific to offer (template, resource, deeper conversation), or wants to start a direct relationship.
**Best for:** Instagram Stories, LinkedIn, Threads

**Formula:**
> "DM me [specific word] and I'll send you [specific thing]."
> "If you're dealing with [specific situation], DM me — happy to share what worked."
> "Want the [specific resource]? DM me [word]."

**Examples:**
> "DM me 'CHECKLIST' and I'll send you the template I use."
> "If you're building your first AI project and stuck on [specific problem] — DM me."

**Why it works:** DMs are high-intent, high-value interactions. The keyword trigger makes the automation easy and the specificity makes the offer feel personal.

**When NOT to use:** As a generic "DM me if you have questions" — this is too vague and invites low-quality interactions.

---

### 6. Click / Visit CTA
**When to use:** There's a specific resource, product, or page to direct people to.
**Best for:** Instagram Stories (link sticker), LinkedIn (link in comments), TikTok bio link

**Formula:**
> "Full breakdown in the link in bio."
> "Link in comments — I wrote the full story there."
> "Swipe up for the template."

**Important notes:**
- LinkedIn: never put links in the post body (60% reach reduction) — always in comments
- TikTok: link in bio only — no clickable links in captions
- Instagram feed posts: link in bio reference ("link in bio for the full list")
- Instagram Stories: use link sticker directly

---

## CTA Placement on Threads

Always the last line of the post. One CTA only.

For threads (multi-post): CTA on the final post only. Earlier posts should end with a natural transition to the next post, not a CTA.

---

## When to Use No CTA

Some posts don't need a CTA. A strong opinion or sharp observation can stand alone — ending with a question weakens it.

Use no CTA when:
- The post ends with a clear conclusion that speaks for itself
- The post is a quick take (1-3 lines) — adding a question makes it feel forced
- The question you'd ask is too obvious or doesn't have a real range of answers

Let the post breathe. Not every post needs explicit engagement prompting.

---

## What Never Works on Threads

- "Like if you agree" — algorithmically penalized, actively reduces reach
- "Drop a ❤️ if this resonated" — same, penalized
- "Follow me for more" — signals desperation, rarely converts
- "Let me know your thoughts in the comments below!" — reads as AI, too generic
- "Hope this was helpful!" — not a CTA at all
- Any URL in the post body — kills reach; if linking is needed, put it in the first reply
- Multiple CTAs — dilutes both, reads as AI-generated
