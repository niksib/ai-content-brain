# Hot Take

## What this angle is

One strong claim that goes against the consensus in the creator's space. Stated flat, without softening. Either stands alone or is followed by a short defense — never a long one.

The reader's reaction should be one of: "yes, finally someone said it", "no, that's wrong because…", or "wait, is that actually true?". Any of these produces a comment. Neutrality kills the angle.

## Signal in the dump

The dump contains a Hot Take if the user:
- States an opinion that contradicts what's normally said in their space ("everyone says X but actually Y")
- Pushes back on advice they've been hearing ("I keep hearing this but it's wrong")
- Names something most people don't admit out loud
- Has a position they hold strongly enough that they expect pushback

Phrases to look for: "actually", "real reason is", "no one says this but", "most people get this wrong", "I disagree with", "the truth is", "everyone seems to think".

## Structure

A Hot Take is short by nature. Length is not a rule — it's a consequence of how the angle works. Diluting a strong claim weakens it.

**Variant A — Standalone claim (1 sentence)**

Just the claim. Nothing else. Works when the claim is so direct it carries the post by itself.

```
[The claim, stated flat]
```

**Variant B — Claim plus one beat of defense (2-3 sentences)**

Claim, then one sharp piece of evidence or one extension. Never a full argument — the post invites the argument to happen in the comments.

```
[The claim, stated flat]

[One specific reason, observation, or extension]
```

That's it. No third paragraph. No "what do you think". No CTA.

## Examples that work

These are real Threads posts. Note how they all hit hard in the first line and either stop or follow with one beat.

**Standalone claim:**

> The smartest people do nothing for hours everyday

> You're not lazy. You just have kids.

> The fastest way to grow is to actually talk to people.

> No one at your funeral is going to say, "She was so responsive on Slack."

> One of the weirdest parts of healing? Realizing you were actually the stable one the entire time. You were just reacting to unstable shit.

**Claim plus one beat:**

> Your life is your responsibility. No one is coming. The moment you take responsibility for yourself is the moment you take your power back.

> Quit your job. Build a startup. Chase your dreams. That's how my savings went from 20k to 0

> A calm relationship isn't boring. You're just addicted to chaos.

> Not all women want Chanel, diamonds, or expensive cars. Some of us just want a soft life, paid bills, and no one bothering us.

> You need to write more. Without AI. Without templates. Without knowing what you're writing about. Just you, an idea, and enough time to do the difficult cognitive work necessary to reach true understanding.

## Banned openings

These prefixes used to work. They're now overused to the point of being mocked. The claim is stronger without them.

- "Unpopular opinion:"
- "Hot take:"
- "Controversial opinion:"
- "Brutal truth:"
- "Hard truth:"
- "Real talk:"
- "Nobody talks about this but"
- "Hear me out"

If the claim needs one of these prefixes to land, the claim isn't strong enough. State it flat.

## Banned closings

A Hot Take ends on the claim or on the one beat of defense. Anything after that softens it.

- "What do you think?"
- "Agree or disagree?"
- "Am I wrong?"
- "Tell me why I'm wrong" (this used to work, now reads as bait)
- "Just my two cents"
- Any summary of what was just said
- Any apology or hedge ("but maybe that's just me")

The claim does its own work. The post invites a reply by being strong, not by asking for one.

## Anti-patterns

These turn a Hot Take into a weak post:

- **Hedged claim**. "I think maybe sometimes people might be wrong about X." Either you believe the take or you don't. Hedging means you don't.
- **Claim followed by long argument**. The angle is "drop a grenade and walk away". A 6-sentence defense turns it into an essay and kills the conversation.
- **Claim that's actually consensus dressed up as contrarian**. "Hot take: hard work matters" is not a hot take. The claim must actually push against something most people in the space believe.
- **Claim with no real conviction**. If the user said it as a passing thought, not a held belief, the post will read as performance. Hot Take requires actual belief — it's the conviction that makes it hit.
- **Performing contrarianism**. Taking a position just because it's opposite. Readers smell this. The claim must be something the user genuinely thinks is true and underrated.
- **Multiple claims in one post**. One Hot Take per post. Two claims dilute each other and the reader doesn't know which to react to.

## Self-check before saving

Read the draft and ask: **if a stranger read this, would they have an immediate reaction — agree, disagree, or pause?** If the answer is "they'd nod and move on", the claim isn't strong enough. Sharpen it or pick a different angle.
