# Identity Snapshot

## What this angle is

A short honest description of where the user is right now — emotionally, professionally, in life. No explanation of why. No journey. Just: this is the state. The post acts like a flag planted in the ground.

The reader's reaction is "this is exactly me" or "I know this feeling". Identity Snapshot works because honesty about a present state is rare on social media — most posts either celebrate progress or perform struggle. Stating a state without polishing it (in either direction) reads as real.

## Signal in the dump

The dump contains an Identity Snapshot if the user:
- Describes how they feel right now
- Names a phase or condition they're in
- Says something honest about themselves that isn't tied to a result
- Speaks from inside a state, not about a state they overcame

Phrases to look for: "right now", "currently", "I'm at the point where", "lately", "I'm just", "I just want", "all I want is".

## Structure

Identity Snapshot is the shortest angle on the list. The honesty does all the work. Length kills it — the more lines, the more it sounds curated.

**Variant A — Single line (1 sentence)**

The state, stated. Nothing else.

```
[The state — short, flat, no qualifier]
```

**Variant B — State plus one line (2 sentences)**

The state, then one extending line that sharpens it without explaining it.

```
[The state]

[One short line — a consequence, a small detail, a reframe — never an explanation]
```

## Examples that work

**Single line:**

> i have zero followers which means this is basically a diary

> Nobody is more tired than a girl in her 20s trying to heal, soft-girl her life, make money, find love, and stay sane all at the same time.

> I cannot be the only one that went from gifted to girlboss to burn out to audhd discovery and now at 40 has zero interest in performing, success, hitting goals or anything. I just want to lay down and feel the sun on my skin.

> My executive function has three modes:
> 1. CEO of a Fortune 500
> 2. Intern on day one
> 3. Cat walking across keyboard

**State plus one line:**

> Called in "sick" today. Actually just needed a break from pretending to be neurotypical.

> Nobody warns you how quickly you go from "Please hire me" to "Fck this job."

> I'm 48. After 20 years in corporate, I learned the hard way.

## Banned openings

Identity Snapshot shouldn't announce that a state is being shared. That kills the immediacy.

- "Just being real for a sec…"
- "Real talk:"
- "Vulnerable post incoming:"
- "Not gonna lie…"
- "Honestly,"
- "Can I be honest?"

The honesty is the post. Don't pre-frame it.

## Banned closings

The snapshot ends on the state or on the single extending line. Anything more turns it into a journey.

- "But I'm working on it."
- "Tomorrow is a new day."
- "We'll get through this."
- "Anyone else?"
- A pivot to advice
- A pivot to gratitude

The angle requires sitting in the state. Pivoting away from it breaks the post.

## Anti-patterns

- **State plus solution.** "I'm exhausted. Here's what's been helping me…" — the moment the post offers a fix, it's no longer a snapshot. Identity Snapshot is the photo, not the recovery plan.
- **State that's actually a flex.** "I'm so blessed and grateful and humble" — disguised humblebrag. Reader sees through it. The angle requires real states, including unflattering ones.
- **State that's actually a complaint.** Long catalogue of grievances. The angle isn't venting — it's naming a state cleanly. One line of complaint is fine; a paragraph of it isn't this angle.
- **Performed vulnerability.** Saying something hard for the optics of saying it. Readers detect this. The state has to be one the user is genuinely in, not one chosen for relatability.
- **Generic state.** "I'm tired" — too broad. The state needs at least one specific edge to it: who, when, what kind of tired.
- **State that turns into a lesson.** "I'm exhausted, and that's taught me…" — the moment the snapshot moves into "and what I've learned", it's a Reframe, not a snapshot.

## Self-check before saving

Read the draft and ask: **am I sitting in the state, or already explaining it away?** If there's any "but" or "however" pivoting the post toward recovery or insight — cut everything after it. The snapshot ends where the explanation begins.
