## Content Strategist Agent — What to Prioritize Per Platform

### Engagement Benchmarks (2026)
- LinkedIn: ~6.2% median engagement — highest of all platforms
- Instagram: ~5.5% (but Reels measured by reach, not followers)
- TikTok: ~4.6%
- Threads: ~3.6% (growing, young platform)

### Best Format Per Platform
| Platform | Best Format | Why |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok | Video | 77% higher engagement than carousels |
| Instagram | Carousel for engagement, Reels for reach | Carousels get 1.92% engagement, Reels reach 2.25x more people |
| Threads | Video (5.55%) > Images (4.55%) > Text (2.79%) | Despite being "text-first", visuals dominate |
| LinkedIn | Carousel/PDF (6.6%) | Highest engagement of ANY format across ALL platforms |

### Content Worth Posting — Decision Signals
- Personal stories and real experiences outperform generic advice on every platform
- Authentic > polished on TikTok and Threads
- Educational frameworks > opinions on LinkedIn
- Saves and shares signal algorithm value more than likes
- Replying to comments in first 60 minutes boosts reach 2x on every platform

### Posting Frequency
- TikTok: 3-5x per week
- Instagram: 2-3 Reels + 1-2 Carousels per week
- Threads: daily or near-daily (text is fast to produce)
- LinkedIn: 3-5x per week max (quality over quantity)

## Threads Agent

### What Actually Works (2026 data)
- **Video posts: 5.55% engagement** — nearly double text (2.79%)
- **Image posts: 4.55%** — outperform text by 60%
- Text posts: 2.79% — still work when hook or opinion is very strong
- Conversation-first content wins — questions, opinions, hot takes
- Authentic, unpolished voice beats corporate tone

### Format Decision Framework (use this when assigning format to a Threads idea)

**`text_post`** — when the value is entirely in the words:
- Strong opinion or hot take where an image would distract
- Relatable observation ("we all do this")
- Vulnerable personal statement
- Any idea where the argument IS the content

**`text_with_image`** — when a single image adds proof or context:
- Showing a concrete result (screenshot, metric, before/after in one frame)
- Personal photo that adds authenticity to a story
- Something visually surprising that triggers "wait, what is that?"
- The image must ADD information the text cannot convey — not just decoration

**`image_series`** — when the idea is a visual story or process:
- Behind the scenes / how something was built or created
- Journey with multiple steps (before → during → after)
- Visual breakdown ("here's everything that went into X")
- Tutorial with visual examples
- Rule: 3–6 images, each must add a new piece of information

**Key question before assigning**: Does the visual add information the text can't convey, or is it just decoration? Decoration → `text_post`. Evidence or story → `text_with_image` or `image_series`.

### Algorithm Signals (confirmed by Mosseri)
- Reply depth is top-tier ranking signal
- Respond to replies within first hour — your own replies count as engagement
- Use ONE topic tag per post
- Engagement bait is actively penalized
- Links now perform BETTER than before (17% better than text-only) — reversed earlier penalty
- Originality prioritized over recycled content

### Hook Patterns That Work
- Bold statement or contrarian opinion
- Short question that creates curiosity gap
- Timely commentary on what happened today/this week
- Personal story opener (mid-scene, no preamble)
- Identity hook ("If you're a creator who...")

### Post Structure
- First line = hook (must stop scroll)
- Keep conversational, not polished
- End with question or invitation to reply
- 500 character limit — be tight
- Never explicit engagement bait ("like if you agree")

### What to Avoid
- Copy-pasting from Instagram or X without rewriting
- Corporate tone
- Overly polished content
- Explicit engagement farming

## Video Agent

### TikTok — Algorithm Rules (2026)

**Primary ranking signal: Completion rate**
- 2026 threshold raised to **70%** for viral distribution (was 50%)
- For a 60-second video: ~42 seconds of average watch time required
- Every second must serve a purpose — no padding

**Hook Rules (first 1-3 seconds)**
- 71% of viewers decide in first 3 seconds whether to stay
- Hook types that work:
  - **Contrarian Hook**: "Everyone says X. Here's why that's destroying your reach."
  - **Question Hook**: Specific frustration or knowledge gap ("Why do some TikToks go viral at 200 views?")
  - **Pattern Interrupt**: Something visually or verbally unexpected
  - **Bold Claim**: Specific, not vague ("Most creators kill their reach with captions")
- Avoid vague openers like "You're doing TikTok wrong" — too generic

**Optimal Length**
- Entertainment/challenges: 15-30 seconds (highest completion)
- Tutorials/how-to: 60-180 seconds
- Storytelling: 60-90 seconds
- Rule: TikTok rewards RETENTION not length — only go long if content earns it

**TikTok as Search Engine**
- 40% of Gen Z use TikTok instead of Google
- Use keywords in spoken dialogue (auto-transcribed) AND on-screen text
- 3-5 strategic hashtags (avoid generic #fyp)
- For search-optimized content: 60-90 seconds minimum

**Content Style**
- Raw and authentic outperforms polished production
- Real person talking to camera builds more trust than b-roll only
- Original audio/voiceover now preferred over trending sounds
- 85% watch without sound — captions are mandatory

**Posting Cadence**
- 3-5x per week minimum for algorithm to understand niche

### Instagram Reels — Algorithm Rules (2026)

**Primary ranking signal: Sends per reach**
- How often viewers DM the Reel to someone else
- Different from TikTok's completion focus — create "share with a friend" content

**Algorithm Differences vs TikTok**
| Signal | TikTok | Instagram Reels |
|---|---|---|
| Primary metric | Completion rate (70%+) | Sends per reach |
| Discovery | Interest graph (non-followers) | Social graph + Explore |
| Style | Raw, authentic | More polished acceptable |
| Audience | Younger, Gen Z | Broader (25+) |
| Virality | Easier for small accounts | Harder, follower count matters more |

**Hook Rules (same 3-second window)**
- Same hook formulas as TikTok apply
- Visual quality matters more than TikTok
- First frame should be high-contrast, readable on mobile

**Content Integration**
- Reels feed into entire Instagram ecosystem (Stories, Explore, profile)
- CTA should point to profile, Stories, or DMs — not just passive view
- Best for: educational content, tutorials, behind-the-scenes, product demos

---

### Script Structure (Both Platforms)
```
[0-3 sec] HOOK — stop the scroll
[3-15 sec] SETUP — deliver on hook promise, establish stakes
[15-end] PAYOFF — value delivery, keep pace tight
[Last 3 sec] CTA — what to do next (follow, comment, DM)
```

**Delivery Notes**
- Speak faster than feels natural — dead air kills retention
- On-screen text to reinforce key points
- Captions always (85% watch muted)
- B-roll keeps visual pace up during voiceover

## Instagram Agent

### Carousel Posts (2026)

**Why Carousels Win**
- 1.92% average engagement — highest format on Instagram
- Gets shown TWICE by algorithm: if user scrolls past, Instagram re-shows starting from slide 2
- Carousels with music pushed into Reels feed = double distribution
- Each swipe = distinct engagement signal to algorithm

**Optimal Structure**
- **Slides: 8-10** is the sweet spot (10-slide carousels hit 2%+ engagement)
- Engagement dips at slides 3-7, then rises again — need strong content throughout
- Slide 1 carries 80% of the weight

**Slide 1 — Hook Formulas**
- Information gap: "The thing no one tells you about X"
- Contrarian: "Stop doing X. Here's why."
- Specific outcome: "How we grew from 1K to 25K in 6 months"
- Identity: "If you're a [specific person] who struggles with X"
- You have 0.25 seconds — bold text, high contrast, immediate clarity

**Slide Structure**
- One idea per slide (flashcard principle)
- Open loop technique: pose question on slide 1, answer on slide 5-6
- "Swipe" prompts mid-carousel to maintain momentum
- Last slide: single clear CTA ("Save this", "Send to [person]", "DM me X")

**Design Rules**
- 4:5 portrait (1080x1350px) — takes more screen space
- Consistent brand colors/fonts across all slides
- Critical elements within center 1080x1080 square (grid cropping)
- Mixed-media (images + video slides) gets 2.33% vs 1.80% image-only

**Metrics to Optimize**
- Swipe-through rate (target: 50%+)
- Save rate (target: 5-10%)
- Completion rate (reaching final slide)

### Instagram Stories (2026)

**Purpose**: Drive profile visits, DMs, and deeper engagement from existing followers

**What Works**
- Quick tips and behind-the-scenes (not polished production)
- Interactive elements: polls, questions, sliders — people are primed to tap
- Story sequences (3-5 stories that tell one arc)
- Day-in-life moments — authenticity over production

**Story Arc Structure**
```
Story 1: Hook — question or bold statement
Story 2-3: Value delivery
Story 4-5: CTA — DM, link, profile visit, poll
```

**Text Overlay Rules**
- Keep text short and bold (Stories watched 2-3 seconds each)
- Use branded stickers, polls, or question boxes for interaction
- Place text in center — avoid edges (Instagram UI overlap)

## LinkedIn Agent

### What Actually Works (2026 data)

**Format Rankings by Engagement**
1. Document/Carousel (PDF): **6.6%** — THE format to prioritize
2. Short native video (30-90 sec): ~5.5%, growing fastest
3. Image + text: ~4.8%
4. Text-only: ~2% — only when writing is exceptionally strong

**Critical Algorithm Rules**
- **Saves** are the #1 signal — 5x more reach than a like
- **Comments** worth 2x more than likes
- **Dwell time** is primary ranking factor — content must make people stop and read
- **First 60-90 minutes** determine 70% of reach — engage fast with commenters
- **External links** reduce reach 50-60% — never put links in post body
- **AI-generated formulaic content** gets 47% less reach (LinkedIn detects it)
- **Engagement bait** algorithmically penalized

### Hook Types (first 3 lines = ~200 characters)
LinkedIn users must click "see more" — those 3 lines determine everything.

**Three hooks that dominate in 2026:**
- **Contrarian Hook**: Challenge a commonly held professional belief
  - "Everyone in X is obsessed with Y. Here's why Z actually works better."
- **Data Hook**: Lead with a hard number that implies a story
  - "We analyzed 10,000 X. The data shows something surprising."
- **Narrative Hook**: Start in the middle of a story
  - "I almost [dramatic thing] yesterday. Then [reversal]."

### Post Structure
```
[Hook — 3 lines, no "see more" needed to get curious]
[1-2 line break]
[Delivery — paragraphs, each 1-3 lines max]
[White space between every paragraph — mobile reading]
[End with question or single clear CTA]
```

### What to Avoid
- External links in post body
- More than 5 hashtags (68% reach reduction)
- Tagging more than 5 people
- Generic AI-sounding language
- Engagement bait ("Comment YES if you agree")
- Posting more than once per day

### Content That Performs
- Personal stories with professional lessons
- "I made a mistake, here's what I learned"
- Specific data or results with your analysis
- Frameworks and step-by-step processes (people save these)
- Honest takes on industry trends
- Behind-the-scenes of building or decision-making

### LinkedIn Carousel (PDF) Best Practices
- Follow same narrative arc: hook slide → value slides → CTA slide
- 8-12 slides optimal
- One idea per slide
- Conversational writing — not slide deck corporate language
- End with strong CTA tied to one action
